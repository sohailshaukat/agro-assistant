Here to talk about various types of soils found in India

Alluvial soil

Alluvial soil deposited from the rivers, winds, glaciers, and sea waves are called the alluvium and soil after this made up the alluvial soil.

Red Soil

Red soils develop under very low rainfall condition on granite and geneses rocks. The dissemination of red oxides of iron gives the characteristic red color of the ground.

Wanna know more?
Black Soil

Black soils regular is clayey, becomes very sticky when wet. Its particular merit lies in its water holding capacity.

Wanna know more?
Mountain soil

Mountain soil is formed mainly due to the deposition of organic matter provided by the forests.

Wanna know more?
Laterite soil

Laterite soil is a kind of clayey rock or soil formed under high temperature and high rainfall. By various modifications, laterite converted into red colored lateritic soils charged with iron nodules.

Wanna know more?
Desert soil

The sand in the desert areas is partly of local origin and partly being blown in from the Indus Valley. It includes even the wind-blown loess.
